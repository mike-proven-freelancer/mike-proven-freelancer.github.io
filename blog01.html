Upwork Reverse Engineered Job Request

My first project, completely AI generated, I have questions...

TL;DR - For everyone not reading the whole blog article:

I had AI build a data analyst project for me so I can learn and have an idea of how to do it for myself in the future. This is my first project taken from a job ad on Upwork and reverse engineered so I can learn from a real world problem, in this case converting social media into revenue.

I had AI generate raw and messy data to extrapolate into a SQL database. The AI also generated the schema for the database and gave me the visual results and explanation of what kind of decisions should be made based on this data. I had AI generate the whole thing, and in the process, it might have given me basic templates to do this in the future, but this wasn’t the learning experience I had imagined.

Firstly, I asked for a lot more messy data than what was given, so I guess the generated messy data that was given are just examples of messy data. Secondly, with everything being automated now, it’s really difficult to understand how this ‘messy data’ was cleaned, structured, and organized by the schema, and how it got some of the information to produce the final phase of the project, visualizations and actionable decisions.

I learned more about social media marketing from this project than actually how to be a data analyst (the real goal), only to discover that the majority of income comes from sponsorship, so it seems really important to see who the sponsors are of your given influencer, because that’s really who they are most loyal to, economically speaking.

My questions though:

1) Are there any long time data analytics folks out there who’ve been using AI to do their analysis? How accurate does it put everything together, how much does it hallucinate or possibly change the values?

2) How do you check the accuracy of your data, what works for you?

3) Ultimately as a service, we are trying to give our employers/contracts/stakeholders information for decision making. When I was studying this in the university as well as in the Google Cert. Program (part of why I stopped) is that data is kind of ephemeral and ‘fudge-able’ and we can be offering bunk and not even realize it, or worse, as a data analyst you can be intentionally giving our sources of income bunk to favor what they want to hear or insights that are either already known or not actually there.

Excuse my cynicism, but data analysts in some regard could be no better than paying a psychic hotline, so how can you prove validity to yourself and others because the data we pick could be bunk from the beginning, we could go into it asking the wrong questions and that will shape the data narrative into bunk, there’s a lot of factors at play where a person with integrity might second guess their information. A better question would also be how can stakeholders determine the data is solid, the right questions are being asked, etc.

So, I guess my third question is what has kept me from really going forward as a data analyst. How do you check and validate the authenticity and integrity of the analysis?

I don’t want to offer this as a service, but it interests me. Strategy and insight and sources of wisdom interest me. I’m also willing to do this for someone because I have the same concerns as my stakeholders and wouldn’t want to mislead anyone, based on my own integrity. So, how can I prove it to both set myself and future employers at ease.

Yeah I didn't use AI to write this blog post, it’s just done candidly and from my normal thinking/speaking voice, casual and maybe unprofessional, but sincerely me.
